  1. Dynamic Flexibility - Strength & Flexibility is an important part of training for runners no matter the race distance they specialize or race in. My coaches at the University of Redlands taught me and my team these exercises. Post collegiately, I have continued to do these exercises 2-3 times per week especially when consitsntly training. They help loosen joins and prepare muscles for running. Try My Dynamic Flexibility Exercises!
  2. Distance Lifting Circuit - I created this workout circuit by combining lifts and exercises I was coached and exercises I added. I believe these exercises help strengthen hips, quads, calves, biceps, triceps, and help increase range of motion. These exercises were integral in my Marathon Training and I recommend adding some of them when doing Distance and Marathon Training. Try My Distance Lifting Circuit
  3. Ab Workouts & Circuits - I do lots of different exercises for abs, obliques, and core. I recommend adding any number of these exercises to strengthen abs or core. Start with 4-6 minutes of continuous abs (1 minute per exercise) and work up to 8-10 minutes if possible. Try My 6-10 Minute Ab Circuit
