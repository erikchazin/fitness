MARATHON TRAINING 

If you are a novice or beginner runner and especially if you have never ran a full Marathon before, I definitely recommend sticking to a training program that is easy to follow and does not 
require you to completely transform your life to make time for runs and workouts. Lots of beginner or first time Marathoners follow programs with 2-3 rest days or easy days each week. If your goal is to run a Marathon just to finish it, there are many beginner Marathon Training books and online training programs that you can follow. Here are a few books and websites that I recommend to help with Marathon Training for runners & marathoners of any tenure or level:

Hal Higdon's Training Program (16 Week Trainng Program)
Nike Running Marathon Training Program
Boston Marathon Training Program
TCS New York City Training Program
Running My First Marathon
Run Fast, Eat Slow

2017-2018 Distance & Marathon Training
Races
*I registered for 7 races for my 2017-2018 Distance & Marathon Training Season in August of 2017 when I began thinking about my goal to run a Marathon. I chose to run the 2018 Phoenix Marathon as my first Marathon because of a few reasons. When planning to run a Marathon (especially if it's your first career Marathon), I recommend taking a few things into consideration; weather, course, race atmosphere, expense, and training.
I chose to run in the Phoenix Marathon in February 2018, because the race was taking place in Arizona which is extremely hot for most of the spring and summer and is often over 100 degrees (in my opionion from April through October). I wanted my peak Marathon training mileage to be about 4 weeks or 1 month before the Marathon, and I wanted to run my first Half Marathon about 6-8 weeks before my Marathon to give myself enough rest and recovery time between my Half Marathon and full Marathon. I knew that the bulk of my training and racing would be December 2017-February 2018, so I wanted to find races in Arizona that were during the same timeframe to fit with the weather and my training which is why I ran the Half and full Marathon that I ran.
One of my large goals was to qualify for the 2019 Boston Marathon, which meant that I had to run in a B.A.A. Certified Marathon and beat my time qualification for my age and gender. My time qualification for my gender and age group of Male 18-35 was 3:05:00. My goal was to break 3 hours and run under 2:59:59. The Phoenix Marathon is a B.A.A. certified course, is considered a Boston Qualifying Marathon, and is known as a fast Marathon course usually with great weather.

MY 2017-2018 DISTANCE & MARATHON TRAINING

Phase Training

*DISCLAIMER: The Marathon Phase Training below is a training program that I CREATED MYSELF based on MY experience with running and my body's reaction to workouts, mileage, and long runs. This training program is not approved by any certified running coaches, sports doctors, or running groups. This training progam is solely recomendations based on what has worked for me and what I have used to coach other Distance and Marathon runners.

For most of my 2017-2018 Marathon Training, I followed a 3 Phase Marathon program. These 3 phases were developed and created based off of how I believe my body reacts to running, mileage, training, workouts, and races. The goal of these 3 phases were to transition my body to be able to run higher mileage and to peak with my best performances during big Long Run and Race goals.

I do believe this 3 phase training program worked well for me but WAS NOT PERFECT. I do not believe there is one single perfect workout or training plan for any runner; I believe that it takes time and practice to be able to understand what each athlete or runner's body reacts to.

3 Phase Marathon Training Program


Phase 1

Base Training, Distance & Recovery Runs, Build Mileage, Strength & Conditioning, Lifting, and Nutrition.

Phase 2

Distance Training, Build High Mileage, Lots of Strength & Conditioning, Stretching & Flexibility Work, Rolling Out, & Nutrition.

Phase 3

Half & Full Marathon Peak Mileage, Hard Strength & Conditioning, Focused Flexibility Work, & Roll Out, Build Long Runs & practice race plans, nutrition, and fuels, Half & Full Marathon Tapers Included.
